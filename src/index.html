What is Lymphatic Drainage

  • Manual lymphatic drainage is a light skin-stretching massage that helps promote the movement of lymphatic fluid out of the swollen limb.
  • It is intended to stimulate lymph nodes and increase rhythmic contractions of the lymphatics to enhance their activity so that stagnant lymphatic fluid can be rerouted.
  • It is composed of four main strokes: circles, scoop technique, pump technique, and rotary.
  • The therapy is applied to your unaffected areas first, making it possible for the fluid to move out of the affected area, or decongest the region.
  • It is blood flow in deep and superficial veins.
  • Help detoxify your body and boost your immmunity by helping white blood cells production.
